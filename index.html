About me

I'm Vincenzo Colella, an Artificial Intelligence and Robotics Engineer, proudly holding a Master's degree from Sapienza University in Rome. Currently, I thrive as a Data Consultant at Argusa SA in Lausanne, Switzerland, wearing the hats of a Data Engineer and Data Scientist. My journey is fueled by a passion for machine learning, neural networks, cloud computing, data, and computer vision. With fluency in Italian, French, English, and Portuguese, my international adventures have finely tuned my adaptability and communication prowess. My master's thesis, a collaborative effort with the Italian National Institute of Nuclear Physics, delved into accelerating Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRIs) through groundbreaking neural network architectures. Certified as a Tableau Desktop Specialist and Snowflake SnowPro Core, I've sprinkled my expertise in prestigious programs like the Oxford Machine Learning Summer School and the Machine Learning Summer School at Nanyang Technological University.

My educational background

  • Master's Degree

    I had my master degree in Computer Engineering specializing in AI & Robotics. My master's thesis, which is available on GitHub, presents an architecture that I designed using neural networks and preprocessing methods to accelerate the acquisition of magnetic resonance images. Specifically, it focuses on undersampling and reconstructing fully sampled multicoil brain MRIs.

  • Bachelor Degree

    I had my bachelor degree in Computer Science at Sapienza University of Rome, and completed it in 2020, after spending one semester in Prague for an Erasmus Program. My Bachelor thesis was on a NLP architecture that could classify reviews from 1 to 5 stars based on sentiment.

  • Erasmus Program - CVUT

    I spent a semester studying at the Czech Technical University of Prague, where I not only perfected my English skills, but also had the opportunity to collaborate with international colleagues on various group projects.

  • Scientific High School

    I earned my high school diploma with a specialization in scientific subjects at Convitto Nazionale Roma. During my studies, I also completed coursework in French and English, culminating in earning the ESABAC (French High School Diploma) in addition to the Italian diploma.
